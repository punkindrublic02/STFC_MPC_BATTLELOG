# STFC-Tool User Guide

STFC-Tool is an independent community battle-analysis tool for **Star Trek Fleet Command**.

It helps players review captured battles, compare crews, inspect officer activity, analyze damage and repairs, and ask questions about their battle history through the website or ChatGPT.

> STFC-Tool is currently available through limited beta access.

\---

## What STFC-Tool Can Do

STFC-Tool can help you:

* Review recent battles
* Compare different crew setups
* See which officer abilities activated
* Analyze damage dealt and damage received
* Review shields, hull damage, mitigation, critical hits, and repairs
* Compare similar battles against the same hostile or target
* Evaluate observed crew and officer performance
* Review hostile abilities and combat mechanics
* Analyze solo and group armada participation
* Ask questions about your captured battles through ChatGPT
* Review older battle history when archived data is available

The tool uses actual captured battle evidence. Results may vary based on your ship, tier, level, officers, research, buildings, technology, target, and the number of comparable battles available.

## Connecting STFC-Tool to ChatGPT

Approved users may be able to connect STFC-Tool to ChatGPT as a custom app.

You will need:

* Approved STFC-Tool access
* The official STFC-Tool connection address
* Your individual authorization information
* A supported ChatGPT plan or workspace
* Developer Mode enabled in ChatGPT when required

The official connection address and setup instructions will be provided privately through Discord.

Never copy connection credentials into a public Discord message, GitHub issue, Twitch chat, screenshot, or social-media post.

### General Setup

After approval:

1. Open ChatGPT.
2. Go to **Settings**.
3. Open **Apps**.
4. Enable Developer Mode if required.
5. Choose the option to create or add a custom app.
6. Enter the official STFC-Tool app name and connection address.
7. Complete authorization using the private information provided to you.
8. Save the app.
9. Start a new ChatGPT conversation.
10. Select STFC-Tool from the available apps or tools.

The exact wording of these settings may vary by ChatGPT plan or workspace.

## Your ChatGPT Conversation Is Private

Each user's ChatGPT conversation is separate.

Another STFC-Tool user cannot see:

* Your ChatGPT conversation
* Your ChatGPT history
* Your personal instructions
* Your ChatGPT memory
* The conclusions discussed in your chat

Multiple approved users may contribute battle evidence to the same STFC-Tool community database. Additional users improve the available comparisons by adding more ships, crews, targets, and battle outcomes.

The conversations themselves are not combined.

## How to Test a Crew Properly

For a useful crew comparison:

1. Use the same player.
2. Use the same ship.
3. Use the same target type and level.
4. Avoid changing research, ship tier, or technology during the test.
5. Change only one officer or position at a time.
6. Run several battles with each setup.
7. Compare damage, rounds, hull loss, repairs, and officer triggers.

One battle is usually not enough to prove that a crew is better.

## Understanding Recommendations

STFC-Tool may use several types of evidence:

### Observed Battle Facts

Information captured directly from battle logs.

### Game Reference Information

Publicly available information about ships, officers, hostiles, abilities, and other STFC mechanics.

### Player and Ship Context

Available information about ship level, tier, officers, research, buildings, and other modifiers.

### Comparisons

Results from battles that are similar enough to compare.

### Inferences

A conclusion based on the available evidence when the exact answer is not directly recorded.

Recommendations are more reliable when:

* Several comparable battles are available
* The same ship and target are used
* Only one test variable changes
* The battle capture is complete
* Current player and ship information is available

\---

## Known Limitations

* Results depend on the quality of captured battle data.
* Short battles may not provide enough evidence.
* Missing battle events can affect calculations.
* Player research and technology information may be incomplete or outdated.
* A crew that works for one player may not perform the same for another.
* Some battles may not have enough comparable samples.
* Game updates can temporarily affect parsing or identification.
* Archived searches may take longer than recent-battle searches.
* ChatGPT may need to use several tool calls for complex questions.
* Recommendations cannot guarantee a specific battle result.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is STFC-Tool official?

No. It is an independent community project.

### Is STFC-Tool free?

Beta access terms and availability will be explained in Discord.

### Can I use the website without ChatGPT?

Yes. ChatGPT access is optional.

### Does another user see my ChatGPT conversation?

No. ChatGPT conversations remain separate.

### Does adding more users improve the tool?

It can improve community comparisons by adding more observed battle evidence. It does not merge or train on users' ChatGPT conversations.

### Can STFC-Tool guarantee that a crew will work?

No. It provides evidence and recommendations based on available data.

### Why can I not see my latest battle?

The capture may not be running, the battle may still be processing, the player link may be incorrect, or the battle may not have been fully captured.

### Where do I request access?

Join the Discord and create a request in **#request-access**.
